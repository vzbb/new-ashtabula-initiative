## 1. Problem Statement

### Core Problem
Tourists visiting Ashtabula's historic sites lack guided context and immersive storytelling. Current options include:
- Static signage with limited information
- Occasional in-person guided tours (scheduling-dependent)
- Generic travel apps with no local depth
- Word-of-mouth recommendations

### Pain Points
| Pain Point | Impact |
|------------|--------|
| No audio narration at 19 covered bridges | Visitors drive by without understanding significance |
| Limited UGRR (Underground Railroad) context | Missed educational opportunity at Hubbard House |
| No unified Harbor District experience | Bridge Street "America's Main Street" winner underutilized |
| Seasonal staff limitations | Sites like Hubbard House only open Memorial Day–Labor Day |
| No self-guided lighthouse access | Ashtabula Lighthouse visible but not interpretable |

### Market Gap
There is **no dedicated mobile walking tour app** for Ashtabula County historic sites. Existing solutions (GPSmyCity, VoiceMap) have minimal Ashtabula coverage and lack:
- Local voice talent/narration
- UGRR-specific historical depth
- Covered bridge architectural context
- Lake Erie maritime history integration

## 5. User Personas

### Persona 1: "Curious Caroline" — The Tourist
- **Demographics:** 35-55, suburban, college-educated, $75K+ household income
- **Context:** Visiting Ashtabula for wine trail weekend with partner/friends
- **Goals:** 
  - Discover hidden gems between winery stops
  - Instagram-worthy experiences
  - Learn enough to feel "in-the-know"
- **Pain points:** 
  - Doesn't know which bridges are worth stopping for
  - Wants context without reading lengthy plaques
  - Limited cell service in rural areas
- **App usage:** Weekend trips, 2-3 tours per year, shares on social media
- **Quote:** *"I drove past those bridges before I realized they were historic. A quick audio guide would have made me stop."*

### Persona 2: "History Hank" — The Local History Buff
- **Demographics:** 55-75, retired or semi-retired, lifelong Ashtabula resident
- **Context:** Active in historical society, gives occasional tours
- **Goals:**
  - Deep historical knowledge, accuracy important
  - Wants to share stories with visitors
  - Supports local preservation efforts
- **Pain points:**
  - Frustrated by historical inaccuracies in generic sources
  - Wants to contribute/correct content
  - Concerned about sites being forgotten
- **App usage:** Regular use, advocates to visitors, submits corrections
- **Quote:** *"I've been telling the Hubbard House story for 20 years. This app could reach people I'd never meet otherwise."*

### Persona 3: "Teacher Tina" — The Educator
- **Demographics:** 30-50, middle/high school history teacher
- **Context:** Planning field trips, integrating local history into curriculum
- **Goals:**
  - Engaging content for students (grades 6-12)
  - Aligned with state history standards
  - Safe, supervised exploration options
- **Pain points:**
  - Limited bus funding for distant field trips
  - Needs pre-visit and post-visit materials
  - Parent permission/compliance concerns
- **App usage:** School group licenses, 2-3 field trips per year
- **Quote:** *"The Underground Railroad is in our standards, but Hubbard House is only open summers. An app would let us 'visit' year-round."*

### Persona 4: "Senior Sam" — The Accessible Traveler
- **Demographics:** 65+, mobility considerations, visiting family in Ashtabula
- **Context:** Wants to explore independently, needs accessibility features
- **Goals:**
  - Self-paced exploration
  - Clear audio (hearing aid compatible)
  - Bench/rest stop information
  - Large text options
- **Pain points:**
  - Walking difficulty on uneven surfaces
  - Audio too fast or too quiet
  - Small text on signs
- **App usage:** Leisurely pace, prefers seated listening, shorter tours
- **Quote:** *"I love local history but can't walk as far as I used to. Knowing where benches are would help me plan."*

## 8. Technical Feasibility Assessment

### Technology Stack (MVP)

| Component | Technology | Rationale |
|-----------|------------|-----------|
| **Frontend** | React PWA | Cross-platform, offline-capable, no app store gatekeeping |
| **Backend** | Firebase (Google) | Auth, Firestore, hosting, serverless functions |
| **Maps** | Mapbox GL JS | Custom styling, offline tile caching, competitive pricing |
| **Audio** | Web Audio API + IndexedDB | Offline playback, background audio |
| **TTS** | ElevenLabs API (optional) | High-quality narration fallback |
| **Geolocation** | Navigator Geolocation API | GPS-triggered content |

### Technical Risk Assessment

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| GPS accuracy in urban canyon (Harbor) | Medium | Medium | Use geofencing with generous radius (~50m) |
| Offline audio storage limits | Low | High | Compress to 64kbps AAC, ~5MB per 30min tour |
| iOS PWA limitations | Medium | Medium | Encourage "Add to Home Screen", document limitations |
| Mapbox API costs at scale | Low | Medium | Implement caching, monitor usage, fallback to OSM |
| Content accuracy disputes | Medium | Medium | Partner with ACHS for review, disclaimer policy |

### Development Complexity: MEDIUM
- **PWA approach** avoids native app complexity
- **Firebase** reduces backend development
- **Existing libraries** (Mapbox, React) accelerate UI
- **Content creation** (audio/scripts) is primary bottleneck
