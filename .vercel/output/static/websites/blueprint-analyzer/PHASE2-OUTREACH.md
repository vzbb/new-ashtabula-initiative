## 3. Discovery Call Questions

### Section A: Current Workflow (5 min)
1. Walk me through your current process for getting measurements from blueprints — from receiving plans to final bid submission.
2. What software tools (if any) do you currently use for blueprint review or measurements?
3. How many hours per week would you estimate your team spends on manual takeoffs or blueprint analysis?
4. Who on your team typically handles blueprint measurements? Is it you, an estimator, or someone else?

### Section B: Pain Points (5 min)
5. What's the most frustrating part of your current blueprint workflow?
6. Have you ever had a project go wrong because of a measurement error or blueprint misunderstanding?
7. How do you handle blueprint revisions when the architect sends updated plans?
8. What's your biggest challenge when reviewing plans on a mobile device or in the field?

### Section C: Current Solutions (3 min)
9. Have you tried tools like PlanGrid, Bluebeam, or Fieldwire? What made you continue or stop using them?
10. What would it take for you to switch from your current process to a new tool?
11. At what price point would a blueprint analysis tool be a "no-brainer" versus "need to think about it"?

### Section D: Desired Features (5 min)
12. If you could wave a wand and automate one part of blueprint analysis, what would it be?
13. How important is it to export measurements directly to Excel or QuickBooks?
14. Would you trust AI-extracted measurements, or would you always double-check them?
15. Do you need to share blueprint analysis with clients, or is this internal-only?

### Section E: Closing (2 min)
16. Would you be willing to test a beta version of our tool with a few of your project blueprints?
17. Can I follow up with you in 2-3 weeks to share what we learned from other contractors?
18. Who else in your network should I talk to about this?

## 6. Risk Assessment Matrix

### Outreach Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| Low response rate (<20%) | Medium | High | Expand to 50-mile radius; use LinkedIn; offer incentive ($25 Amazon card for call) |
| Negative feedback on concept | Low | High | Pivot to adjacent market (inspectors, architects); document objections |
| Can't reach decision-makers | Medium | Medium | Use general inquiry lines; visit in-person; attend trade events |
| Price sensitivity higher than expected | Medium | High | Test multiple price points; explore freemium model |
| Technical skepticism ("AI won't work") | Medium | Medium | Offer live demo; show competitor success stories; start with simple use case |
| Seasonal timing (winter slowdown) | Low | Low | Contractors plan spring projects in winter — actually good timing |

### Business Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| Competitor launches similar tool | Low | High | Speed to market; focus on local relationships; lower price point |
| AI accuracy insufficient | Medium | Critical | Manual QA in early versions; set clear expectations; hybrid approach |
| Market too small | Low | Medium | Expand to Cleveland metro; adjacent trades (electrical, plumbing) |
| Customer acquisition cost too high | Medium | High | Partner with suppliers for distribution; referral program |

### Risk Scoring

**Overall Risk Level: MODERATE**
- Primary concern: AI accuracy and customer acquisition
- Mitigation strategy: Start with narrow use case (square footage only), expand based on validation
