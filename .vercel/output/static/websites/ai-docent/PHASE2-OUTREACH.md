**Subject:** Free AI docent for your museum — no cost, unlimited visitors

```

**The challenge we see:**
Small museums like yours do incredible work preserving local history, 
but you're limited by volunteer schedules and static signage. Visitors 
often arrive when no docent is available, or they want deeper 
information than signs can provide.

**Our solution — AI Docent:**
A smartphone-based AI guide that visitors access by scanning a QR 
code at your entrance. It provides:

• Conversational exhibit information (visitors ask questions, get 
  detailed answers)
• On-demand tours in multiple languages
• Audio descriptions for vision-impaired visitors
• Sign language video integration for deaf visitors
• Works 24/7 — no volunteer schedule needed
• Completely FREE for your museum

**How it works:**
1. We create AI content based on your exhibits and artifacts
2. You place QR codes at key locations (we provide printable signs)
3. Visitors scan and chat with the AI docent
4. You get analytics on what visitors ask about most

**What we've learned from research:**
- Visitors want to ask questions, not just read plaques
- Multilingual support opens your museum to new audiences
- Accessibility features help you meet grant requirements
- Staff saves time answering repetitive questions

**Real example:**
At the Perambulator Museum, a visitor could ask "Why did Victorian 
parents use perambulators instead of strollers?" and get a detailed 
answer about 19th-century parenting practices — even if no volunteer 
is present.

**No cost, no risk:**
• Free setup and hosting for 2 years
• We handle all technical work
• You control all content
• Cancel anytime
• No hardware to buy or maintain

**Next step:**
Would you be open to a 20-minute conversation about your museum's 
needs? No commitment required — just exploring whether this would 
help your visitors.

Best regards,
```

## 3. Museum Content Requirements

### Per Museum Setup Checklist

**Content Needed:**
- [ ] High-quality artifact/exhibit photos (10-20 key items)
- [ ] Written descriptions/history for each featured item
- [ ] Frequently asked questions (from volunteer experience)
- [ ] Interesting stories/anecdotes about exhibits
- [ ] Connection to broader history (local, state, national)
- [ ] Related exhibits at other local museums

**Technical Setup:**
- [ ] QR code placement strategy (entrance, key exhibits)
- [ ] WiFi signal strength testing (or offline mode)
- [ ] Staff/volunteer briefing (what to tell visitors)
- [ ] Feedback mechanism (simple form or verbal)

**Accessibility Considerations:**
- [ ] Audio description scripts for key visual elements
- [ ] Sign language video for main introduction
- [ ] Large print QR codes for low-vision visitors
- [ ] Wheelchair-accessible QR placement

### Content Development Process

```
Week 1: Museum visit + documentation
  - Photography of key exhibits
  - Interview volunteers/staff
  - Collect existing materials

Week 2: Content creation
  - Write artifact descriptions
  - Develop conversational responses
  - Create FAQ database

Week 3: AI training + testing
  - Feed content to AI system
  - Test Q&A accuracy
  - Refine responses

Week 4: Deployment
  - Install QR codes
  - Train staff
  - Soft launch with feedback

Week 5+: Iteration
  - Monitor analytics
  - Add content based on visitor questions
  - Refine based on feedback
```

## 4. Competitive Intelligence

### Smartify
- **Pricing:** $5K-20K+/year
- **Target:** Major museums (Smithsonian, Louvre)
- **Weakness:** Too expensive for small museums
- **Our angle:** Free tier for small institutions

### Guidekick / Antenna Audio
- **Pricing:** $10K-50K+ setup
- **Target:** Large institutions
- **Weakness:** Enterprise-only, overkill for small sites
- **Our angle:** Simple, affordable, AI-powered

### VoiceMap / izi.TRAVEL
- **Pricing:** $300-500/tour or freemium
- **Features:** Static audio, no AI
- **Weakness:** Not interactive, generic platform
- **Our angle:** Conversational AI, museum-specific

### Custom GPTs
- **Pricing:** Free/$20/mo
- **Weakness:** Requires technical knowledge, no location awareness
- **Our angle:** Plug-and-play, no tech expertise needed

**Key Differentiation:**
- **Affordable:** Free tier for small museums
- **AI-powered:** Conversational, not just audio playback
- **Accessible:** Multilingual, audio description, sign language
- **Network effect:** Cross-promotion between local sites
- **Analytics:** Data on what visitors care about
- **No hardware:** Smartphone-based, QR code access

## 5. Risk Assessment Matrix

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| Museums skeptical of technology | Medium | HIGH | Start with one enthusiastic partner |
| Poor cell/WiFi coverage | Medium | MEDIUM | Offline mode, booster installation |
| Content accuracy concerns | Medium | HIGH | Museum approval process, disclaimers |
| Low visitor adoption | Medium | MEDIUM | Clear signage, staff promotion |
| AI hallucination/wrong answers | Low | HIGH | Constrained AI, museum-reviewed content |
| Sustainability after grant | Medium | MEDIUM | Subscription model, ongoing grant strategy |
