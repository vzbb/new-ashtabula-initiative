## Program Inventory

### 1. SNAP (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program)

**Administered by:** Ohio Department of Job & Family Services  
**Local Office:** Ashtabula County Job & Family Services

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Purpose** | Food assistance via EBT card |
| **Gross Income Limit** | 200% FPL (e.g., $2,608/mo for 1 person, $3,526/mo for 2) |
| **Net Income Limit** | 100% FPL (e.g., $1,304/mo for 1 person) |
| **Asset Limit** | **No asset limit in Ohio** |
| **Max Benefits** | $298 (1 person), $546 (2 people), $785 (3 people), $994 (4 people) |
| **Minimum Benefit** | $24 (1-2 person households) |
| **Key Deductions** | 20% earned income, dependent care, shelter/utilities, medical (60+) |
| **Utility Allowances** | Heating/Cooling: $766, Basic: $479, Electric: $108, Phone: $46 |

**Required Documents:**
- Valid Ohio ID or driver's license
- Social Security numbers (all household members)
- Proof of income (past 30 days, 90 days, or 12 months depending on program)
- Utility account numbers
- Rent/mortgage information

**Application:** Online at [Benefits.Ohio.gov](https://Benefits.Ohio.gov) or in-person at County Job & Family Services

### 2. WIC (Women, Infants, and Children)

**Administered by:** Ohio Department of Health (ODH)  
**Local Provider:** ACCAA WIC Program  
**Phone:** 1-800-686-1754

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Purpose** | Nutrition for pregnant/postpartum women, infants, children under 5 |
| **Income Limit** | 185% FPL (e.g., $2,413/mo for 1 person, $3,260/mo for 2, $4,109/mo for 3) |
| **Asset Limit** | None |
| **Auto-Qualify** | SNAP, Medicaid, or TANF recipients |
| **Special Note** | Pregnant women count unborn child as household member |

**Monthly Benefits:**
- **Pregnant/Postpartum:** $47 produce + milk, eggs, cheese, cereal, formula
- **Breastfeeding:** $52 produce + same as above (no formula if fully breastfeeding)
- **Infants:** Formula, cereal (6+ months), baby food
- **Children (1-5):** $9 produce + milk, eggs, cheese, juice

**Required:**
- Proof of income, identity, residency
- Health records for children
- In-person or virtual certification appointment
- Nutritional risk assessment (free, includes height/weight, blood test)

**Recertification:** Every 6-12 months

### 3. HEAP (Home Energy Assistance Program)

**Administered by:** Ohio Development Services Agency  
**Local Provider:** Ashtabula County Community Action Agency (ACCAA)  
**Info Line:** 1-800-282-0880

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Purpose** | One-time annual payment toward heating/cooling bills |
| **Income Requirement** | Must meet income eligibility guidelines (specific thresholds not found) |
| **Application Period** | Typically opens in fall for winter heating season |
| **Who Can Apply** | Homeowners and renters |

**Where to Apply:**
- Online: [energyhelp.ohio.gov](https://energyhelp.ohio.gov)
- Local: ACCAA offices
- Also available at: Job & Family Services, Senior Centers, Area Agencies on Aging

**Related Program:** PIPP (Percentage of Income Payment Plan) for ongoing utility bill assistance

### 4. ACCAA Core Programs

**Organization:** Ashtabula County Community Action Agency  
**Website:** [accaa.org](https://accaa.org)  
**General Info:** Dial 211

| Program | Services | Notes |
|---------|----------|-------|
| **Emergency Assistance** | Rent, utilities, food, clothing | Case-managed, requires appointment |
| **Head Start** | School readiness for ages 3-5 | Income-eligible, comprehensive services |
| **Utility Assistance** | HEAP, PIPP | Energy bill help |
| **Rental Assistance** | Emergency rent help | Prevents eviction |

**Universal Requirements (ACCAA):**
- Valid Ohio ID or driver's license
- List of all household members + birthdates
- Social Security numbers (all household members)
- Income documentation (past 30 days, 90 days, 12 months varies by program)
- Utility account numbers

**Process:**
1. Application/assessment (in-person, mail, or online depending on program)
2. Case manager reviews documents
3. Eligibility determination
4. If needs exceed program scope → referral to 211
