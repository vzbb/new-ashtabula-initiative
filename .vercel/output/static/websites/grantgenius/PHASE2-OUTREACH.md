## 2. Outreach Email Templates

### Template A: Local Foundation (Ashtabula Foundation, FAO)

**Subject:** Partnership — grant discovery platform for Ashtabula nonprofits

```
Dear [Foundation Director],

I'm writing from a local technology initiative to propose a 
partnership that could help Ashtabula County nonprofits discover 
and secure more funding — including grants from your organization.

**The challenge we see:**
Small nonprofits and businesses in our county struggle to find 
grants. They don't know about funding sources like yours, miss 
deadlines, and lack the resources of larger organizations with 
dedicated grant writers.

The result? Under-resourced community organizations and missed 
opportunities to strengthen our county.

**Our solution — GrantGenius:**
A free, Ashtabula County-focused grant discovery platform that:
• Aggregates federal, state, and local funding opportunities
• Sends deadline alerts so organizations never miss applications
• Provides eligibility checkers ("Do I qualify for this?")
• Offers application templates and guidance
• Is completely FREE for all Ashtabula County users

**Why partner with [Foundation Name]:**
• Increased awareness of your grants among eligible organizations
• Better-prepared applicants (higher quality submissions)
• Data on funding gaps in our community
• Recognition as a community resource leader
• No cost or technical burden to your foundation

**What we'd include about your foundation:**
• Complete grant program listings
• Eligibility requirements in plain English
• Application deadlines with alert options
• Contact information and guidance
• Success stories (if you share them)

**What we need from you:**
• Up-to-date grant program information
• Deadline calendar (if available)
• Feedback on platform design
• Optional: mention in your communications

Would you be open to a 20-minute conversation about how this 
could serve our shared mission of strengthening Ashtabula County?

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Phone]
[Email]
```

## 3. Grant Database Structure

### Categories to Track

| Category | Examples | Source Types |
|----------|----------|--------------|
| **Nonprofit Operations** | General operating, capacity building | Foundations, United Way |
| **Program-Specific** | Youth services, food security, arts | Federal, state, foundations |
| **Capital Projects** | Building improvements, equipment | Federal, foundations |
| **Small Business** | Startup, expansion, innovation | JobsOhio, USDA, SBA |
| **Arts & Culture** | Projects, performances, exhibits | Ohio Arts Council, foundations |
| **Economic Development** | Business attraction, workforce | State, local |
| **Education** | Scholarships, programs, facilities | Foundations, federal |
| **Emergency Assistance** | COVID relief, disaster recovery | Federal, state, ACCAA |

### Data Fields for Each Grant

```
Grant Opportunity:
├── Basic Info
│   ├── Title
│   ├── Funder (name, type, contact)
│   ├── Amount (min/max/average)
│   └── Deadline (rolling/fixed/cyclical)
├── Eligibility
│   ├── Organization type (501c3, LLC, etc.)
│   ├── Geographic scope
│   ├── Focus areas
│   └── Special criteria (minority-owned, rural, etc.)
├── Application Details
│   ├── Description
│   ├── Requirements checklist
│   ├── Timeline
│   └── Link to full application
└── Meta
    ├── Date added
    ├── Last verified
    └── Success rate (if known)
```

## 4. Competitive Intelligence

### Instrumentl
- **Pricing:** $50-150/month
- **Strengths:** Smart matching, AI writing, workflow
- **Weaknesses:** Expensive for small orgs, national focus
- **Our angle:** FREE, Ashtabula-specific

### Foundation Directory Online
- **Pricing:** $50-200/month
- **Strengths:** Comprehensive foundation data
- **Weaknesses:** Paywalled, overwhelming, no local focus
- **Our angle:** Free, curated, simple

### GrantStation
- **Pricing:** $199/year
- **Strengths:** Affordable, good coverage
- **Weaknesses:** Limited Ohio/local focus
- **Our angle:** Ashtabula-specific opportunities

### Ashtabula Growth Partnership (indirect)
- **Pricing:** Free
- **Strengths:** Local, trusted
- **Weaknesses:** Business-only, no aggregation
- **Our angle:** Comprehensive (business + nonprofit + arts)

**Key Differentiation:**
- **FREE:** No cost barrier for under-resourced organizations
- **Local:** Ashtabula County focus, not national noise
- **Comprehensive:** Federal + state + local + foundation
- **Simple:** No learning curve, plain English guidance
- **Alerts:** Deadline notifications so nothing is missed

## 7. Go/No-Go Decision Tree

```
After outreach:
│
├─► If 2+ local foundations partner:
│   └─► Proceed to Phase 3 (SPEC.md)
│       └─► Build with funder data
│
├─► If United Way or Chamber partners:
│   └─► Proceed with distribution channel
│       └─► Leverage their network
│
├─► If 10+ nonprofits interested:
│   └─► Proceed with user validation
│       └─► Build for demonstrated need
│
└─► If minimal interest:
    └─► Pause
        └─► Reassess approach
```

### Go Criteria
- [ ] 2+ local foundations agree to data sharing
- [ ] OR United Way or Chamber commits to partnership
- [ ] OR 10+ organizations express strong interest

### No-Go Criteria
- [ ] Funders decline to participate (data access)
- [ ] No distribution partner (United Way/Chamber)
- [ ] Organizations satisfied with current methods
