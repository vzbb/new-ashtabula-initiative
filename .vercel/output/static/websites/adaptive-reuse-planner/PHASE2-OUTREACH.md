## 2. Outreach Email Templates

### Template A: Planning & Community Development

**Subject:** Tool proposal — helping property owners navigate adaptive reuse

```
Dear Planning Department Director,

I'm writing from a local technology initiative to propose a digital 
tool that could reduce your workload while helping property owners 
understand adaptive reuse opportunities in Ashtabula.

**The challenge we see:**
Property owners and small developers often struggle to understand:
• What zoning changes or variances are required for reuse projects
• Which permits are needed and in what sequence
• What incentives (tax credits, grants) they may qualify for
• How to navigate the approval process

This results in repeated inquiries to your office, incomplete 
applications, and frustrated applicants who abandon good projects.

**Our solution — Adaptive Reuse Planner:**
A free web-based tool that helps users:
• Input an address and see current zoning/allowed uses
• Explore conversion options (retail→housing, warehouse→office, etc.)
• Generate a personalized permit checklist
• Calculate potential incentive eligibility
• Find the right contacts for each step

**Benefits to PCD Department:**
• Fewer basic "what's allowed here?" inquiries
• Better-prepared applicants with complete information
• Increased compliance (owners understand requirements upfront)
• Support for downtown revitalization goals
• Free tool — no IT burden on city staff

**What we'd need:**
• Access to zoning code (public) and zoning map data
• Permit requirement documentation
• Input on common pain points you see
• 30-minute conversation about current workflow

**Pilot approach:**
We'd start with 3-5 common reuse scenarios (e.g., retail to 
apartments, warehouse to offices) and expand based on feedback.

Would you be open to a brief conversation about this opportunity?

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Phone]
[Email]
```

## 3. Adaptive Reuse Scenario Database

### Priority Scenarios for MVP

| Building Type | New Use | Complexity | Market Demand |
|---------------|---------|------------|---------------|
| **Downtown retail** | Apartments/condos | Medium | HIGH |
| **Warehouse/industrial** | Offices/coworking | Medium | HIGH |
| **Warehouse/industrial** | Maker space/studios | Low | MEDIUM |
| **Church/school** | Community center | Medium | MEDIUM |
| **Gas station** | Retail/restaurant | High | MEDIUM |
| **Bank building** | Restaurant/event space | Medium | MEDIUM |
| **Factory/mill** | Mixed-use | High | LOW |

### Permit Sequence by Scenario

**Example: Retail → Residential Apartments**

1. **Zoning verification** — Is residential permitted? (PCD)
2. **Zoning variance** — If not permitted, apply for variance (PCD)
3. **Site plan review** — Parking, access, landscaping (PCD)
4. **Building permit** — Structural, systems (County Building)
5. **Electrical permit** — Upgrade service (County Building)
6. **Plumbing permit** — Multi-unit systems (County Building)
7. **HVAC permit** — Zoned heating/cooling (County Building)
8. **Fire inspection** — Egress, suppression (County Building)
9. **Certificate of Occupancy** — Final approval (County Building)

**Timeline:** 3-6 months typical
