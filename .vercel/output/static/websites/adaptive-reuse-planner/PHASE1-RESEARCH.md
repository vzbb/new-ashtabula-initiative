**Date:** February 17, 2026  
**Status:** 🟡 Research in Progress

## Problem Statement

Property owners and developers in Ashtabula struggle to understand:
1. What adaptive reuse options are available for existing buildings
2. Which zoning changes/variances may be required
3. What permits are needed for repurposing structures
4. What incentives exist for adaptive reuse projects
5. How to navigate the approval process

## Common Adaptive Reuse Scenarios (Research-Based)

### Building Types → New Uses Matrix

| Original Building | Common New Uses | Key Considerations |
|-------------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Warehouse/Industrial** | Offices, lofts, maker spaces, breweries | High ceilings, large windows, loading docks |
| **Retail/Commercial** | Restaurants, apartments, coworking | Parking requirements, accessibility |
| **School/Church** | Community centers, housing, offices | Historic preservation, large open spaces |
| **Factory/Mill** | Mixed-use (retail + residential + office) | Structural capacity, utilities |
| **Gas Station** | Retail, restaurant, small office | Environmental cleanup (underground tanks) |
| **Bank** | Restaurant, retail, office | Vaults, security features, grand spaces |
| **Hospital/Clinic** | Housing, senior living, offices | Specialized infrastructure, accessibility |
| **Theater** | Performance venue, retail, housing | Acoustics, seating, stage equipment |

### Typical Conversion Requirements

**Structural:**
- Load capacity analysis
- Seismic/structural upgrades
- Floor reinforcement (residential requires higher load)

**Systems:**
- HVAC (zoning for multiple uses)
- Electrical (upgraded capacity)
- Plumbing (residential vs. commercial)
- Fire suppression

**Accessibility:**
- ADA compliance (often grandfathered but required for full renovation)
- Elevator installation (multi-story residential)
- Accessible entrances

**Zoning/Legal:**
- Use change permit
- Variance (if needed)
- Historic preservation review (if applicable)
- Parking requirements

## Incentive Programs Research (Web Sources)

### Federal Programs

#### Federal Historic Rehabilitation Tax Credit (FHTC)
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Credit Amount** | 20% of Qualified Rehabilitation Expenditures (QREs) |
| **Eligibility** | Income-producing historic buildings listed on National Register |
| **Administered By** | National Park Service Technical Preservation Services |
| **Combined Use** | Can be combined with OHPTC for up to 45% total credit |
| **Key Standard** | Must comply with Secretary of Interior's Standards for Rehabilitation |

#### Opportunity Zones (Federal)
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Benefit** | Defer capital gains taxes until Dec 31, 2026 |
| **Additional Benefit** | Reduction in capital gains tax if held 5+ years |
| **Eligibility** | Investment in designated Qualified Opportunity Zones |
| **Ashtabula Relevance** | Parts of Ashtabula may be in designated zones |

### Ohio State Programs

#### Ohio Historic Preservation Tax Credit (OHPTC)
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Credit Amount** | Up to 25% of Qualified Rehabilitation Expenditures (QREs) |
| **Maximum Credit** | $5 million per project (catalytic projects may exceed) |
| **Application Fee** | 0.1% of requested tax credit amount |
| **Schedule** | Bi-annual applications (March and September deadlines) |
| **Administered By** | Ohio Department of Development (ODOD) |
| **SHPO Role** | Reviews historic rehabilitation compliance |
| **Contact** | Lisa Brownell: (614) 752-2345, lisa.brownell@development.ohio.gov |

#### Ohio Brownfield Remediation Program
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Purpose** | Grants for cleanup/remediation of brownfield sites |
| **Eligibility** | Properties with known/potentially hazardous substances |
| **Ashtabula Example** | Carlisle-Allen Building received $812,900 (2022) |
| **Recent Awards** | Ashtabula County Port Authority: $600,000 for Kunkle building (2024) |

#### Ohio Opportunity Zone Tax Credit
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Purpose** | State incentive complementing federal Opportunity Zones |
| **Benefit** | Additional state income tax credit for OZ investments |
| **Administered By** | Ohio Department of Development |

### Regional/Local Programs

#### NOACA Brownfields Revolving Loan Fund
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Coverage** | 12-county Northeast Ohio region (includes Ashtabula) |
| **Funding Type** | Low-interest loans and sub-grants |
| **Administered By** | Northeast Ohio Areawide Coordinating Agency (NOACA) |
| **Use** | Cleanup of hazardous substances or petroleum |

#### Ashtabula County Land Bank
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Purpose** | Acquire and repurpose vacant/abandoned properties |
| **Potential Use** | Property acquisition for adaptive reuse projects |

### Incentive Stacking Strategy

Properties may qualify for **multiple incentives simultaneously**:

**Historic Building Example:**
- Federal Historic Credit: 20%
- Ohio Historic Credit: 25%
- **Total Potential: 45% of qualified costs**

**Contaminated Historic Building:**
- Federal Historic Credit: 20%
- Ohio Historic Credit: 25%
- Brownfield Remediation Grant: Variable
- **Total Potential: 45%+ of project costs**

**Opportunity Zone + Historic:**
- All historic credits
- Capital gains deferral
- Ohio Opportunity Zone credit
- **Maximum financial leverage**
