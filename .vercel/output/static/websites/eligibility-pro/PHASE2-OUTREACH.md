### Ohio SNAP (Food Assistance) — Oct 2025 - Sept 2026

**Gross Income Limits (200% FPL)**

**Maximum Benefit Amounts**
| Household Size | Monthly Benefit |
|----------------|-----------------|
| 1 | $298 |
| 2 | $546 |
| 3 | $785 |
| 4 | $994 |
| 5 | $1,183 |
| 6 | $1,421 |
| 7 | $1,571 |
| +Each | +$218 |

**Key Rules:**
- **Asset Limit:** None in Ohio (no countable resource test)
- **Senior/Disabled:** Expanded eligibility if over 60 or disabled
- **Work Requirements:** Able-bodied adults 18-49 without dependents have work requirements
- **Utility Allowances:** Heating/Cooling $766, Basic $479, Electric/Gas/Water $108 each

**Deductions Allowed:**
- Standard deduction: $209-$299 (based on household size)
- 20% earned income deduction
- Dependent care costs
- Court-ordered child support
- Medical expenses (over $35 for seniors/disabled)
- Shelter expenses (rent/mortgage + utilities, minus 50% of income after other deductions)

**Source:** https://www.snapscreener.com/guides/ohio

## 5. Key Pain Points to Validate

Questions to ask during stakeholder interviews:

1. **For ACDJFS:**
   - What % of calls are basic eligibility questions vs. complex cases?
   - How often do applicants arrive unprepared (missing documents)?
   - Would pre-screened applicants improve efficiency?

2. **For 211/ACCAA:**
   - What are the top 5 reasons people call 211?
   - Do callers often not know what programs they qualify for?
   - How much time is spent explaining eligibility vs. making referrals?

3. **For Legal Aid:**
   - Do clients miss benefits that could stabilize their situation?
   - Are benefit denials/appeals a significant part of your caseload?
   - Would better screening reduce emergency legal needs?

4. **For Caseworkers:**
   - What intake bottlenecks exist in current workflows?
   - Would automated document checklists save time?
   - Privacy/HIPAA concerns with third-party tools?
