## 2. Outreach Email Templates

### Template A: City Engineering Division

**Subject:** Proposal — AI assistant to reduce resident engineering questions

```
Dear Engineering Director,

I'm writing from a local technology initiative to propose a tool 
that could reduce your office's workload while helping residents 
better understand engineering and permit requirements.

**The challenge we see:**
Your office receives countless calls from residents asking basic 
questions: "Do I need a permit for my driveway?" "Who handles 
sidewalk repairs?" "What's the process for sewer connection?"

These calls take staff time, often during busy periods, and many 
residents are confused about the difference between City, County, 
and Zoning requirements.

**Our solution — Engineers Assistant:**
An AI-powered conversational tool that:
• Answers common engineering questions 24/7
• Clarifies City vs County vs Zoning jurisdiction
• Guides residents through permit requirements
• Provides step-by-step process information
• Is completely FREE for the City and residents

**Example interactions:**
Q: "Do I need a permit to replace my driveway?"
A: "If you're replacing the surface on private property, no 
permit is needed. However, if you're modifying the curb cut 
(access to the street), you'll need a Curb Cut Permit from 
City Engineering. Call (440) 992-7111 or visit City Hall."

Q: "My yard floods — what can I do?"
A: "Private drainage solutions (French drains, regrading) on 
your property typically don't need permits. However, connecting 
to the public storm system requires Engineering approval. Also, 
you cannot divert water onto a neighbor's property. For specific 
guidance, contact Engineering at (440) 992-7111."

**Benefits to Engineering Division:**
• Reduced phone calls for common questions
• Better-informed residents (fewer incomplete applications)
• 24/7 availability (residents get answers evenings/weekends)
• Consistent information (reduces miscommunication)
• No IT burden — we host and maintain

**What we need:**
• Accurate information about common procedures
• Permit requirement documentation
• Feedback on typical resident questions
• 30-minute conversation about current workflow

**Content control:**
You review all AI responses for accuracy before launch. The tool 
always directs complex situations to your office with clear 
contact information.

Would you be open to a brief conversation about this opportunity?

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Phone]
[Email]
```

## 4. Jurisdictional Clarity Framework

### Decision Tree for Users

```
Starting project...
    ↓
Is it on your private property only?
    ├── YES → Check if building permit needed (County)
    │         └── Also check Zoning for setbacks
    └── NO (involves street/utility/right-of-way)
              └── Contact City Engineering

Building permit needed?
    ├── YES → 1. Get Zoning Permit (City)
    │         2. Get Building Permit (County)
    │         3. Schedule inspections
    └── NO → Check Zoning for setbacks/compliance

Involves digging?
    └── YES → Call OHIO 811 first (1-800-362-2764)
```

## 5. Technical Implementation Notes

### Data Sources to Integrate

| Source | Information | Access |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| **City website** | Engineering forms, contacts | Public |
| **County website** | Building permit info | Public |
| **Zoning code** | Setbacks, requirements | Public PDF |
| **OHIO 811** | Digging safety | API available |
| **Staff input** | Common questions | Interview |

### AI Training Approach

1. **Knowledge Base Creation**
   - Compile Q&A pairs from research
   - Organize by category and jurisdiction
   - Include official citations

2. **Conversational Design**
   - Friendly, jargon-free language
   - Step-by-step guidance
   - Always provide contact info for complex cases

3. **Safety Controls**
   - Clear disclaimers on every answer
   - Direct to official sources for final decisions
   - Flag when professional consultation needed

4. **Feedback Loop**
   - Users can flag incorrect/outdated info
   - Regular review with City/County staff
   - Quarterly updates
