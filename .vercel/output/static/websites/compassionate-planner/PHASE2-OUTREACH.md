## 3. DISCOVERY CALL QUESTION GUIDES

### Guide A: Funeral Director Discovery (30 minutes)

**Opening (3 min)**
- "Thank you for taking the time. How has your week been?"
- "Before we dive in, could you tell me a bit about [Funeral Home Name] and your role there?"
- Set context: "I'm researching how to better support families through digital tools"

**Understanding the Current State (10 min)**

1. **Family Preparation**
   - "When families first contact you, how prepared are they typically?"
   - "What percentage of families have done any pre-planning?"
   - "What's the biggest surprise families have about the process?"

2. **Pain Points**
   - "Walk me through what happens when a family arrives without any plans in place."
   - "Where do families get stuck or overwhelmed during arrangements?"
   - "What questions do you answer over and over?"
   - "What takes up most of your time that could be automated or streamlined?"

3. **Pre-Planning**
   - "How do you currently approach pre-planning conversations?"
   - "What objections do people have to pre-planning?"
   - "What motivates people who DO pre-plan?"

4. **Communication**
   - "How do you coordinate with out-of-town family members?"
   - "What tools do you use to communicate with families?"
   - "How do families prefer to receive information?"

**Exploring Opportunities (10 min)**

5. **Digital Tools**
   - "Are you currently using any software for case management?"
   - "What technology has helped your business most?"
   - "What frustrates you about current technology options?"

6. **Wish List**
   - "If you could wave a magic wand, what would make serving families easier?"
   - "What would help families arrive more prepared?"
   - "What would make pre-need sales easier?"

7. **Local Market**
   - "How has the funeral industry changed in Ashtabula over the past 10 years?"
   - "What do families here value most?"
   - "How do you differentiate from competitors?"

**Validation & Partnership (5 min)**

8. **Reaction to Concept**
   - "I'm imagining a simple digital tool that helps families organize their wishes 
     and documents before they need them. What would you think about that?"
   - "Would you find value in families arriving with their preferences documented?"
   - "What concerns would you have about referring families to a digital tool?"

9. **Partnership Interest**
   - "If this tool existed and was well-designed, would you consider sharing it with families?"
   - "What would need to be true for you to feel comfortable recommending it?"
   - "Would you be interested in a pilot partnership to test this with select families?"

**Closing (2 min)**
- "Is there anything I should have asked that I didn't?"
- "Who else should I talk to in the community?"
- "Can I follow up with you as I develop this further?"
- "Thank you so much for your time and insights."

**Key Signals to Listen For:**
- Specific complaints about current processes (pain = opportunity)
- Enthusiasm about pre-planning growth
- Mention of out-of-town children coordinating arrangements
- Frustration with "educating" unprepared families
- Interest in efficiency/competitive advantage

### Guide B: Hospice Staff Discovery (30 minutes)

**Opening (3 min)**
- "Thank you for making time. I know how demanding your work is."
- "Could you share a bit about your role and how long you've been with [Organization]?"
- Set context: "I'm exploring whether digital tools could help families during care transitions"

**Understanding Family Needs (10 min)**

1. **Care Transitions**
   - "Walk me through what typically happens when a patient transitions to hospice care."
   - "At what point do families typically start thinking about funeral arrangements?"
   - "Who usually makes those decisions in the families you serve?"

2. **Family Stress Points**
   - "What are families most worried about during end-of-life care?"
   - "Where do you see families struggle most with decisions?"
   - "How do family dynamics play into decision-making?"

3. **Information Gaps**
   - "What do families wish they knew before reaching this point?"
   - "What resources do you find yourself explaining repeatedly?"
   - "What do families not know to ask about?"

4. **Current Resources**
   - "What tools or resources do you currently provide to families?"
   - "What works well? What falls short?"
   - "How do you handle after-hours questions or needs?"

**Exploring Digital Opportunities (10 min)**

5. **Technology in Care**
   - "How comfortable are the families you serve with technology?"
   - "Do you currently use any digital tools for family communication?"
   - "What barriers do you see to families using digital planning tools?"

6. **The Bridge**
   - "How do you currently help families transition from medical care to funeral planning?"
   - "Is there a gap between when you discharge and when funeral homes take over?"
   - "What would a smooth handoff look like?"

7. **Ideal Support**
   - "If you could give every family one resource at the start of care, what would it be?"
   - "What would help families feel more in control during this time?"

**Partnership Exploration (5 min)**

8. **Collaboration Potential**
   - "How do you currently partner with community organizations?"
   - "Would your organization be open to piloting a digital planning resource with select families?"
   - "What would need to be true for your organization to feel comfortable referring families to a digital tool?"

9. **Compliance & Ethics**
   - "Are there any regulatory considerations for recommending external resources?"
   - "How do you ensure resources you share are trustworthy?"

**Closing (2 min)**
- "What haven't I asked that I should know?"
- "Who else should I speak with - either here or at other organizations?"
- "Would it be okay to follow up as I develop this further?"

**Key Signals to Listen For:**
- Mention of families "not knowing where to start"
- Frustration with limited after-hours support
- Interest in reducing family anxiety
- References to family conflict during decision-making
- Mention of out-of-town adult children

### Guide C: Family Caregiver Discovery (30 minutes)

**Opening (3 min)**
- "Thank you so much for being willing to talk with me."
- "To start, could you tell me a bit about who you're caring for and your situation?"
- Set context: "I'm trying to understand what would have helped you be better prepared"

**Experience Exploration (10 min)**

1. **The Journey**
   - "Walk me through how you became a caregiver for [parent/loved one]."
   - "What was the hardest part of that transition?"
   - "What do you wish you had known at the beginning?"

2. **Planning Status**
   - "Have you and [loved one] talked about their wishes for end-of-life?"
   - "If yes: How did that conversation go? What prompted it?"
   - "If no: What has prevented that conversation?"

3. **Information Management**
   - "Where do you keep important documents and information about [loved one's] care?"
   - "How do you share information with other family members?"
   - "What information have you had trouble finding when you needed it?"

4. **Resource Discovery**
   - "How did you find the resources you're currently using?"
   - "What resources have been most helpful?"
   - "What have you searched for but couldn't find?"

**Digital Tool Exploration (10 min)**

5. **Current Tech Use**
   - "What apps or digital tools do you currently use for caregiving or organization?"
   - "How comfortable are you with using apps and websites?"
   - "What would make you hesitant to use a new digital tool?"

6. **Wish List**
   - "If you could wave a magic wand, what would make caregiving easier?"
   - "What would help you feel more prepared for what's ahead?"
   - "What would help you have better conversations with [loved one] about their wishes?"

7. **Value Proposition Test**
   - "I'm imagining a simple app that helps you:
     - Keep all important documents in one place
     - Track care information and appointments
     - Share access with family members
     - Learn about local resources
     - Guide conversations about wishes
     What would you think about something like that?"

**Pricing Sensitivity (5 min)**

8. **Willingness to Pay**
   - "If this tool existed and worked well, would you use it?"
   - "What would make it worth paying for?"
   - "If it cost $5/month, would that seem reasonable?"
   - "What if it was free but had ads? Or free for basic, paid for advanced features?"

**Closing (2 min)**
- "What else should I know about what caregivers like you need?"
- "Would you be interested in testing early versions of this tool?"
- "Can I follow up with you as I develop this?"

**Key Signals to Listen For:**
- Emotional relief when describing having things organized
- Frustration with scattered information
- Guilt about not being prepared enough
- Mention of family conflict or communication challenges
- Financial stress related to care costs
- Desire for local, trusted resources

## 6. OBJECTION HANDLING

### Common Funeral Home Objections

**"We already have a website/pre-planning system"**
- "That's great - many homes do. I'm curious: how often do families use it before 
  they need you? What percentage arrive with their preferences documented?"
- "I'm not trying to replace what you have, but rather help families arrive more 
  prepared for the conversation with you."

**"Our families prefer personal contact"**
- "Absolutely, and that's what makes your service special. I'm imagining this as 
  something that helps families organize their thoughts BEFORE they sit down with 
  you - so your personal time is more meaningful."
- "Would you be open to seeing a demo before deciding?"

**"We tried digital tools before and they didn't work"**
- "I'd love to learn from that experience. What went wrong? What would have made 
  it successful?"
- "This is exactly why I'm doing research first - to avoid building something 
  that doesn't work for actual funeral directors."

**"I don't have time for this"**
- "I completely understand. Would a 10-minute phone call work instead? Or I could 
  send you 3 questions via email?"
- "Is there someone else at your organization who handles innovation or pre-planning?"

### Common Hospice Objections

**"We have to be careful about partnerships"**
- "Absolutely, and I respect that. I'm not looking for endorsement - just 
  understanding of what families need. This is purely research at this stage."
- "Would it help to see our privacy policy and data handling practices?"

**"Our families are overwhelmed as it is"**
- "That's exactly why I'm building this - to reduce overwhelm by helping families 
  prepare before crisis hits. Would something that simplified planning be helpful 
  or just add to the noise?"
