## Product Concept

A **simplified Click & Collect platform** connecting Ashtabula County residents with local grocery staples from independent grocers and local food producers. Unlike complex full-grocery apps, this tool focuses on:

- **Curated staples** (eggs, dairy, bread, meat, produce basics)
- **Local-first sourcing** (prioritize independent grocers and county producers)
- **Simplified ordering** (no account required, SMS confirmation, minimal friction)
- **Pickup coordination** (schedule slots, reduce wait times)

### Core Value Proposition
> *"Order your weekly staples from local Ashtabula grocers and producers — simple, fast, and community-first."*

## Market Context: Food Access in Ashtabula County

### Critical Finding: Rural Food Desert Reality
Recent reporting (February 2026) confirms **growing food deserts in Ohio**, particularly in rural areas where residents live **10+ miles from the nearest grocery store**. Key findings:

- Ashtabula County has experienced **independent grocery store closures** (Jefferson Golden Dawn closed 2019 after 43 years)
- Rural residents face transportation barriers to access full-service grocery stores
- Senior citizens (60+) require transportation assistance for grocery shopping (Country Neighbor program exists but is limited)

### Existing Grocery Infrastructure

| Store | Location | Type | Online Ordering | Notes |
|-------|----------|------|-----------------|-------|
| **Giant Eagle** | Geneva | Regional chain | ✅ Curbside + Instacart | 15-20 min from rural areas |
| **ALDI** | Ashtabula | Discount chain | ✅ Instacart only | No native pickup |
| **Meijer** | Ashtabula | Supercenter | ✅ Curbside + delivery | Large footprint |
| **Sander's Market** | Jefferson, Conneaut | Independent | ❌ No online ordering | **Key partner candidate** |
| **Save A Lot** | Ashtabula | Discount | ❌ No online ordering | Locally owned (Nemenz/Kawecki) |

### Key Insight
**Independent grocers like Sander's Market and Save A Lot have NO online ordering presence.** This creates a gap that Local Grocer Go could fill.

## Competitor Analysis

### Direct Competitors (Grocery Ordering)

| Competitor | Model | Strengths | Weaknesses | Ashtabula Fit |
|------------|-------|-----------|------------|---------------|
| **Instacart** | Gig delivery | Broad store coverage, 1-hour delivery | High fees (service + delivery + tip), no local focus, no pickup at independents | ⚠️ Limited to chain stores |
| **Giant Eagle Curbside** | Store-operated | Native integration, established | Giant Eagle only, requires account, 15+ min drive for rural residents | ⚠️ Single chain |
| **Walmart Grocery** | Store-operated | Low prices, app-based | Walmart only, corporate focus, limited local connection | ⚠️ Single chain |
| **DoorDash** | Gig delivery | Restaurant + grocery | High markup, limited rural coverage, no scheduling | ❌ Poor rural fit |

### Indirect Competitors

| Competitor | Model | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|------------|-------|-----------|------------|
| **Phone orders** | Traditional | Personal, no tech barrier | Time-consuming, no confirmation, easy to mess up |
| **Farmers markets** | In-person | Local, fresh | Limited hours, seasonal, no pre-ordering |
| **CSA programs** | Subscription | Direct farm support | Large upfront cost, no choice in products, seasonal only |

### Market Gap Identified
**No tool serves Ashtabula County specifically with:**
1. Aggregated local/independent grocer ordering
2. Simplified, no-account-required experience
3. SMS-based confirmation for low-tech users
4. Local producer integration (eggs, honey, meat from county farms)
5. Senior-friendly interface (large text, simple flow)

## User Personas

### Persona 1: "Rural Rita"
- **Profile:** Lives 12 miles from nearest grocery, limited transportation
- **Pain:** Weekly grocery trip is a 2-hour commitment; forgets items, makes multiple trips
- **Needs:** Simple pre-ordering, pickup scheduling, staple-focused (not full grocery)
- **Tech comfort:** Smartphone user, prefers SMS over apps

### Persona 2: "Senior Stan"
- **Profile:** 72 years old, uses Country Neighbor transportation for shopping
- **Pain:** Physical difficulty walking large stores, limited mobility, forgets list
- **Needs:** Large text interface, minimal steps, reliable pickup time, phone support option
- **Tech comfort:** Basic smartphone, prefers calling but willing to try simple web form

### Persona 3: "Busy Brenda"
- **Profile:** Working parent, wants to support local but time-constrained
- **Pain:** No time to visit farmers markets, forgets CSA pickup windows
- **Needs:** Quick order form, local producer options, flexible pickup times
- **Tech comfort:** High, uses many apps

### Persona 4: "Producer Paul"
- **Profile:** Small egg/meat producer, no e-commerce capability
- **Pain:** Can't compete with grocery stores on convenience
- **Needs:** Simple way to receive orders, no platform fees, direct customer connection
- **Tech comfort:** Moderate, uses Facebook

## Business Model Options

| Model | Description | Pros | Cons |
|-------|-------------|------|------|
| **Community Service** | Free for users and grocers | Maximum adoption, community goodwill | Unsustainable, no revenue |
| **Commission** | 5-10% per order | Sustainable, aligns with volume | Resistance from thin-margin grocers |
| **SaaS Fee** | $49-99/mo per grocer | Predictable revenue, grocer investment | Barrier to adoption for small stores |
| **Hybrid** | Free for producers, $29/mo for grocers, commission >$500/mo | Balanced, scales with success | Complex to communicate |

### Recommendation
Start as **Community Service** (free) with Harbor Gardens as anchor partner. Transition to Hybrid model after proving value.

## Strategic Positioning

|---------|-----------------|-----------|---------------------|
| Independent grocer focus | ✅ Core | ❌ Chains only | ❌ Single chain |
| No account required | ✅ SMS option | ❌ App required | ❌ Account required |
| Local producer integration | ✅ Native | ❌ None | ❌ Limited |
| Senior-friendly design | ✅ Large text/simple | ❌ Complex app | ⚠️ Moderate |
| SNAP/EBT support | ⚠️ Planned | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes |
| Ashtabula-specific | ✅ Primary | ❌ Generic | ❌ Generic |

### Success Metrics
- **3+ local grocers/producers** onboarded within 6 months
- **100+ monthly orders** by month 6
- **25% senior users** (60+) by month 6
- **70% repeat order rate** by month 3

### Hypothesis
> A simplified, local-first Click & Collect platform focused on staple groceries and independent Ashtabula grocers can capture market share from national delivery apps by offering personal service, local producer access, and senior-friendly design that chains ignore.
