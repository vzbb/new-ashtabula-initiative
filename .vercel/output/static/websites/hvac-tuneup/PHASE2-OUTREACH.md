## 2. Outreach Email Templates

### Template A: HVAC Contractor

**Subject:** Free tool to book more maintenance appointments

```
Dear [Contractor Name],

I hope this message finds you well. I'm reaching out from a local 
technology initiative to offer a free tool that could help your 
HVAC business grow maintenance contracts and reduce office busywork.

**The challenge we see:**
Many HVAC companies lose 30-40% of maintenance contract renewals 
because homeowners simply forget to schedule tune-ups. Your office 
staff spends hours playing phone tag with customers.

**Our solution — HVAC Tune-Up:**
A free booking platform that:
• Lets homeowners schedule spring AC and fall furnace tune-ups online
• Sends automatic seasonal reminders (no more forgotten appointments)
• Shows your available slots in real-time
• Handles payments so you get paid faster
• Costs you nothing — we make money from small booking fees

**What we've learned:**
- Homeowners want transparent pricing (no more "call for estimate")
- They forget seasonal maintenance until it's too late
- Emergency repairs cost them 3-5x more than preventive care
- They'll book online if it's easy

**What you get:**
• More maintenance contracts (steady recurring revenue)
• Less phone tag (customers book online)
• Fewer emergency calls (better preventive maintenance)
• Free marketing (platform promotes your business)
• No setup costs, monthly fees, or commitment

**The ask:**
Would you be open to a 15-minute conversation about your current 
booking process? No commitment — just feedback to help us build 
something that actually helps HVAC businesses like yours.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Phone]
[Email]
```

## 4. Competitive Intelligence

### HomeAdvisor
- **Model:** Lead generation, $50-100 per lead
- **Weakness:** Expensive, shared leads (multiple contractors get same lead)
- **Our angle:** Direct booking, exclusive leads, no upfront cost

### Thumbtack
- **Model:** Marketplace with commission
- **Weakness:** Price competition, race to bottom
- **Our angle:** Value-based, maintenance-focused (not emergency)

### Angi (formerly Angie's List)
- **Model:** Subscription + lead fees
- **Weakness:** Generic, national focus, declining reputation
- **Our angle:** Local focus, seasonal specialization, transparent pricing

### Company Websites
- **Model:** Direct booking (rare)
- **Weakness:** No comparison, hard to find, limited features
- **Our angle:** Aggregated comparison, reminders, maintenance tracking

**Key Differentiation:**
- **Seasonal focus:** Spring AC, fall furnace (not just emergency)
- **Transparent pricing:** Upfront rates, no "call for estimate"
- **Maintenance contracts:** Manage recurring service
- **Automatic reminders:** Never miss a tune-up
- **Local only:** Ashtabula County contractors
- **No lead fees:** Small booking fee instead of expensive leads

```
│

    └─► Pause
        └─► Reassess model (maybe homeowner-paid instead?)
```

### Go Criteria
- [ ] At least 3 HVAC contractors commit to pilot
- [ ] OR 1 large contractor (One Hour, etc.) interested
- [ ] OR 20+ homeowners express interest via survey

### No-Go Criteria
- [ ] All contractors prefer phone-only booking

- [ ] Zero interest from both sides
