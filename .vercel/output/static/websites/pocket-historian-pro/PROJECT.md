# PROJECT — Pocket Historian Pro

## Vision
AI-powered, persona-based historical storytelling for Ashtabula museums. Visitors scan QR codes to have conversations with historical figures — lighthouse keepers, ship captains, pioneers — bringing exhibits to life through immersive audio narratives.

## Problem Statement
Small museums like Ashtabula Maritime Museum and Ashtabula County Historical Society have rich stories but limited resources:
- All-volunteer staff can't give personal tours to every visitor
- Static exhibits don't engage younger audiences
- Professional audio guides cost $500-2000+ to produce
- No affordable AI-powered solutions exist for small museums

## Target Users
- **Primary:** Family visitors (parents with children)
- **Secondary:** History enthusiasts and researchers
- **Tertiary:** Casual tourists visiting Geneva-on-the-Lake area

## Value Proposition
"Talk to history, not just read about it." Visitors engage in conversations with historical personas via QR codes, hearing their stories in first-person audio narratives generated by AI and delivered via TTS.

## Competitive Positioning
| Competitor | Their Limitation | Our Advantage |
|------------|------------------|---------------|
| YourAudioTour | No AI/personas, expensive setup | AI-generated persona conversations |
| Nubart | Per-visitor pricing, no conversation | Fixed cost, interactive chat |
| Anne Frank House bot | One-way Q&A | Immersive first-person narratives |
| ChitChatBot.ai | Generic platform | Purpose-built for small museums |

## Market Opportunity
- 25,000+ artifacts at Maritime Museum alone
- 3 Historical Society properties with untold stories
- QR code museum adoption growing 40%+ annually
- No competitor offers persona-based AI storytelling for small museums
