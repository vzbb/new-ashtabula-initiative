# Permit Whisperer — Pitch Document

## One-Liner
AI-guided permit assistant that simplifies the maze of municipal permits — telling citizens exactly what they need, when they need it.

---

## 30-Second Demo Script

**Hook:** "You want to build a deck in your backyard, but you have no idea if you need a permit, which forms to fill out, or how long it will take..."

**Demo:**
1. **(2s)** Open newashtabula.vercel.app/permits/
2. **(10s)** "I tell the AI what I'm building — 'I want to add a 12x16 deck to my home'..."
3. **(10s)** "And it tells me exactly which permits I need, what documents to prepare, estimated timeline, and fees."
4. **(5s)** "I can track my application status and get alerts for any issues."
5. **(3s)** "Permits without the panic."

---

## What It Does
Guides citizens through the permit process with AI-powered assessment, requirement checklists, and real-time status tracking.

---

## Who It's For
- **Primary:** Homeowners doing renovations/improvements
- **Secondary:** Small business owners, contractors
- **Tertiary:** Municipal permit staff

---

## The Problem
Getting permits is confusing:
- Unclear which permits apply
- Complex forms and requirements
- Unknown timelines and fees
- No visibility into status

**Result:** Projects delayed, violations incurred, frustrated citizens.

---

## The Solution
**Permit Whisperer** guides you step-by-step:
- 🤖 **AI Assessment** — Describe your project, get requirements
- ✅ **Smart Checklists** — Documents, inspections, fees
- ⏱️ **Timeline Estimates** — Know what to expect
- 📱 **Status Tracking** — Real-time updates on your application

---

## Key Features
1. **Natural Language Input** — Just describe your project
2. **Jurisdiction-Aware** — Local rules and requirements
3. **Document Checklists** — Nothing forgotten
4. **Fee Calculator** — No surprises
5. **Status Dashboard** — Track multiple permits

---

## Call to Action
**For Municipalities:** "Reduce permit desk calls by 70% with self-service AI guidance."

**For Citizens:** "Stop guessing — get the right permit the first time."
