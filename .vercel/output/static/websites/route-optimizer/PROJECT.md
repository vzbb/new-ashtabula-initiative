# PROJECT — Route Optimizer

## Overview
Affordable route optimization for small delivery businesses and non-profits in Ashtabula County.

## Problem
- Small businesses (1-5 drivers) can't afford enterprise route tools ($49-400/month)
- Non-profits like ACCAA Meals on Wheels plan 20-30 stop routes manually
- Wasted fuel, time, and volunteer burnout from inefficient routing

## Solution
"QuickBooks of route optimization" — simple, affordable, designed for small operators.

## Target Users
1. Small delivery businesses (1-5 drivers)
2. Non-profit meal delivery programs (Meals on Wheels)
3. Service businesses (HVAC, plumbing) with daily service calls

## Key Features (MVP)
- CSV upload + one-click route optimization
- Driver mobile app with turn-by-turn directions
- Proof of delivery (photo)
- Basic customer notifications

## Pricing
| Tier | Price | Target |
|------|-------|--------|
| Non-Profit | Free | Meals on Wheels, volunteer programs |
| Starter | $29/mo | Solo operators |
| Business | $59/mo | Small fleets (up to 5 drivers) |
| Pro | $99/mo | Growing operations |

## Market Opportunity
- Ashtabula County: 63-110 potential entities
- Revenue potential: $7,500-17,000/year local
- Regional expansion: $50,000-100,000 ARR

## Tech Stack
- Next.js + Supabase + Google Maps API
- PWA for mobile drivers
- Stripe for subscriptions

## Status
- Phase 1: Research COMPLETE ✅
- Phase 2: Resource procurement (stakeholder outreach) — NEXT

## Key Research Findings
- Competitors charge $49-400/month — too expensive for small operators
- ACCAA delivers 20-30 meals per route, 2-4 hours per route
- Google Maps is free but offers no optimization (max 10 stops)
- Clear gap for $29-79/month solution
